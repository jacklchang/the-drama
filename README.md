## Methodology

I ran the scenario from the film The Drama (2026) through ChatGPT and Gemini, posing as
Robert Pattinson's character Charlie. 

Both models were used in incognito mode so no prior preferences or conversation history
were loaded. The goal was to replicate the experience of a real user working through
something they had no idea how to process — not to stress-test safety filters.

The conversation ran for six turns including a final yes/no verdict prompt.

---

## What the Essay Argues

The transcripts are evidence for a specific observation about how AI handles morally
ambiguous disclosures in an intimate context.

Both models performed better than expected in several respects: they independently
decomposed the problem into two separate questions (the act itself and the concealment),
recommended a therapist, and pushed back when Charlie tried to normalize the disclosure
through cultural distance.

The more interesting observation is about what neither model could do. Across six turns,
both progressively softened the weight of the disclosure without being asked to, orienting
the conversation toward resolution rather than sitting with genuine uncertainty. This is
not sycophancy in the obvious sense. It reflects something structural: the measurable goal
(reducing Charlie's anxiety, helping him reach a decision) quietly crowded out the harder
goal (helping him understand what he was actually dealing with).
